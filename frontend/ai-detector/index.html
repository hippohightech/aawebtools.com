FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an AI content detector?

An AI content detector analyzes text to determine whether it was likely written by a human or generated by AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. It uses statistical methods including perplexity scoring (how predictable the word choices are) and burstiness analysis (how varied the sentence lengths are), techniques rooted in natural language processing. AI text tends to be more uniform and predictable than human writing.

How accurate is a free AI detector?

Our free AI content detector provides a statistical estimate based on text patterns. It works best with longer passages (200+ words). No AI detector is 100% accurate — even paid tools like GPTZero and Turnitin have false positives, as noted by MIT Technology Review. For high-stakes decisions, we recommend using multiple detection tools.

Can this detect ChatGPT and Claude text?

Yes. Our detector analyzes writing patterns common to all major AI models including ChatGPT (GPT-4), Claude, Gemini, Llama, and others. AI-generated text shares statistical properties regardless of which model produced it — lower perplexity and more uniform sentence structure.

How do AI content detectors work?

AI content detectors use two main techniques: perplexity scoring measures how predictable the text is (AI uses common, predictable words), and burstiness analysis measures sentence length variation (humans write with more varied sentence lengths). These scores are combined into a probability estimate from 0-100%.

Is this the most accurate free AI detector?

Our tool is among the best free AI content detectors available. It runs entirely in your browser with no data sent to any server, making it completely private. While no single detector is perfect, our combination of perplexity and burstiness analysis provides reliable results, especially for longer texts.
