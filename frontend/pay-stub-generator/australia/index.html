PAYG Withholding: How Income Tax Works in Australia

Under the PAYG withholding system, employers deduct income tax from each pay before it reaches the employee. The amount withheld is determined by the ATO's tax tables and the employee's Tax File Number (TFN) declaration, which indicates whether they have claimed the tax-free threshold of $18,200.

For the 2025-26 financial year, the individual income tax rates for Australian residents are:

  • 0% on taxable income from $0 to $18,200 (tax-free threshold)
  • 16% on income from $18,201 to $45,000
  • 30% on income from $45,001 to $135,000
  • 37% on income from $135,001 to $190,000
  • 45% on income above $190,000

These rates reflect the Stage 3 tax cuts that took effect on 1 July 2024, which reduced the 32.5% bracket to 30% and raised the $120,000 threshold to $135,000. Employees who do not provide a TFN have tax withheld at the top marginal rate of 45% plus the Medicare levy.

Superannuation Guarantee (SG)

Superannuation is Australia's mandatory retirement savings system. For the 2025-26 financial year, the superannuation guarantee rate is 12%, up from 11.5% in 2024-25. This is the final step in the legislated increase schedule that began at 9.5%. Employers must pay SG contributions on top of an employee's ordinary time earnings (OTE) to the employee's nominated super fund.

Key super rules for 2025-26:

  • SG rate: 12% of ordinary time earnings
  • Maximum super contribution base: approximately $62,270 per quarter (employers are not required to pay SG on earnings above this cap)
  • No minimum earnings threshold: since 1 July 2022, all employees receive super regardless of monthly earnings
  • Payment deadline: quarterly, within 28 days after the end of each quarter
  • Concessional contributions cap: $30,000 per year (including employer SG, salary sacrifice, and personal deductible contributions)

Super is paid on top of gross wages, not deducted from them. However, it must be clearly shown on pay slips so employees can verify contributions are being made.

Medicare Levy and Medicare Levy Surcharge

The Medicare levy is 2% of taxable income, funding Australia's public health system. It is built into the PAYG withholding amount rather than appearing as a separate line on the pay slip. Low-income earners below approximately $26,000 per year (singles) are exempt or pay a reduced rate through the Medicare levy reduction.

High-income earners without appropriate private hospital insurance may also be subject to the Medicare Levy Surcharge (MLS), which adds 1% to 1.5% depending on income tier. Singles earning above $93,000 and families above $186,000 trigger the MLS if they lack compliant private cover.

HELP/HECS-HELP Repayment

Employees with outstanding Higher Education Loan Program (HELP) debts, including HECS-HELP, FEE-HELP, VET Student Loans, and SA-HELP, make compulsory repayments through the PAYG system. The repayment rates for 2025-26 are progressive, starting at 1% of repayment income above the minimum threshold of approximately $54,435 and increasing to 10% for income above $151,201. Employers withhold these amounts when the employee's TFN declaration indicates an outstanding HELP debt.

Fair Work Pay Slip Requirements

Under the Fair Work Act 2009, Australian employers must issue pay slips within one working day of pay day. A compliant pay slip must include:

  • Employer's name and ABN
  • Employee's name
  • Pay period and payment date
  • Gross and net pay amounts
  • Hourly rate of pay (or annual salary)
  • Hours worked (including overtime, at ordinary and penalty rates)
  • Loadings, allowances, bonuses, incentive payments, and penalty rates itemised separately
  • Deductions (PAYG withholding, salary sacrifice, union fees)
  • Superannuation contributions (fund name, amount paid, and payment period)

The Fair Work Ombudsman can issue fines of up to $16,500 per contravention for individuals and $82,500 for corporations that fail to provide compliant pay slips.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the superannuation guarantee rate in Australia for 2025-26?

The superannuation guarantee rate for the 2025-26 financial year is 12%, up from 11.5% in 2024-25. Employers must pay SG on top of an employee's ordinary time earnings to a compliant super fund. The $450 per month minimum earnings threshold was removed from 1 July 2022, meaning all employees receive super regardless of how little they earn.

How does PAYG withholding work in Australia?

PAYG (Pay As You Go) withholding is the system through which Australian employers deduct income tax from employee wages before paying them. The amount withheld is based on ATO tax tables, the employee's Tax File Number (TFN) declaration, and their claimed tax-free threshold. Employers remit withheld amounts to the ATO, usually quarterly for small businesses or monthly for medium and large employers. Employees reconcile their total tax position when lodging their annual tax return.

What is the Medicare levy and who has to pay it?

The Medicare levy is 2% of taxable income and funds Australia's public healthcare system. Most Australian residents for tax purposes must pay it. Low-income earners below the Medicare levy threshold (approximately $26,000 for singles in 2025-26) are exempt or pay a reduced rate. High-income earners without private hospital insurance may also pay the Medicare Levy Surcharge (MLS) of 1% to 1.5% depending on income. The levy is not shown as a separate deduction on pay slips — it is built into the PAYG withholding amount.
