If you have tried downloading TikTok videos or slideshows recently and found that your go-to tool no longer works, you are not imagining things. The landscape of TikTok downloading changed significantly in late 2025, and the effects are still being felt across every third-party tool in 2026.

The catalyst was TikTok's transition to USDS (United States Data Security) ownership. After years of political pressure, regulatory hearings, and a near-ban that dominated headlines throughout 2024, TikTok's US operations were restructured under a new entity designed to address national security concerns about data sovereignty. The USDS transition was not just a corporate reshuffle -- it involved a fundamental overhaul of TikTok's technical infrastructure in the United States and, by extension, much of its global content delivery network.

For everyday users, the app continued to work seamlessly. But for third-party developers and downloading tools, the transition broke nearly everything. TikTok updated its API endpoints, changed the way video and image files are served from its CDN, rotated authentication tokens more aggressively, and implemented new anti-scraping measures that rendered most existing download tools useless overnight.

The result: by January 2026, an estimated 60% of TikTok downloader tools that existed before the transition were either completely non-functional or returning errors on the majority of URLs. The tools that survived are the ones maintained by active developers who reverse-engineered the new infrastructure and adapted their backends accordingly.

Key Finding

Of the 7 tools we tested in March 2026, 4 work reliably across all TikTok content types including the newer photo slideshows. Two tools have partial functionality, and one is effectively broken. The divide comes down to whether each tool's maintainers adapted to TikTok's post-USDS infrastructure.

This guide is designed to give you a clear, honest picture of what actually works right now. We tested each tool against five different types of TikTok content -- standard videos, slideshows (photo carousels), live replay clips, duets/stitches, and audio-only extraction -- and recorded the results. We also looked at watermark removal, download speed, mobile compatibility, and whether each tool respects your privacy or harvests your data through ads and trackers.

TikTok slideshows deserve special attention. Introduced in 2022 and massively popular since, TikTok's photo carousel feature (also called "photo mode") lets creators post multiple still images that viewers swipe through, set to music. Downloading slideshows is trickier than downloading standard videos because the content consists of multiple individual image files rather than a single video stream. Many tools that handle videos fine will fail entirely on slideshows, convert them to low-quality video compilations, or only grab the first image. We tested specifically for this.

Types of TikTok Content You Can Download

Standard videos

This is the bread and butter of TikTok -- short-form video clips ranging from 15 seconds to 10 minutes. Every tool we tested supports standard video downloads to some degree, though success rates varied widely (from 100% with AAWebTools down to around 33% with MusicalDown). When a tool advertises "TikTok downloader" functionality, this is usually the format they are referring to. Expect HD quality (up to 1080p) from the top-tier tools and SD quality (480-720p) from the rest.

Slideshows (photo carousels)

TikTok's photo carousel feature, launched in 2022, lets creators post a series of still images that viewers swipe through, typically accompanied by a music track. Slideshows have become hugely popular for recipe cards, outfit inspiration, travel diaries, study notes, and aesthetic content. Downloading slideshows is more complex than downloading videos because the content is stored as multiple individual image files rather than a single video stream.

The best tools (AAWebTools, SnapTik) extract each photo as a separate high-resolution image file. Mid-tier tools (SSSTik) convert the slideshow into a compiled video. Weaker tools either grab only the first image or fail entirely. If slideshow downloads are important to you, this distinction should be a primary factor in your tool choice.

Profile photos

Downloading TikTok profile pictures at full resolution is a niche but common request. TikTok displays profile photos at a compressed size, but the original high-resolution image is stored on their servers. Only a handful of tools offer profile photo download functionality. AAWebTools supports this through its TikTok downloader interface -- simply paste the profile URL and select the profile photo option.

Audio extraction

Some users want just the audio track from a TikTok video -- typically to identify a song, save a sound effect, or listen to a voiceover offline. Audio-only extraction downloads the sound as an MP3 file. This feature is supported by AAWebTools, SnapTik, and SSSTik, but not by TikMate, SaveTT, TikDownloader, or MusicalDown.

Duets and stitches

Duets (split-screen collaborations) and stitches (clip-then-respond format) are collaborative TikTok features. When downloaded, they save as a single combined video file that includes both creators' content. Most working tools handle duets and stitches the same as standard videos, though TikMate and MusicalDown struggled with some duet URLs in our testing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it legal to download TikTok slideshows and videos?

Downloading TikTok content for personal, offline viewing is generally considered acceptable in most jurisdictions, similar to saving a webpage for later reading. However, re-uploading downloaded content as your own, using it commercially without permission, or distributing copyrighted material violates both TikTok's Terms of Service and copyright law. Always credit the original creator if you share downloaded content, and never monetize someone else's work without explicit consent.

Can I download TikTok slideshows as individual photos instead of video?

Yes, but only certain tools support this. and SnapTik can extract individual high-resolution photos from TikTok slideshows (also called photo carousels). Most other downloaders convert the slideshow into a single video file with the photos displayed as frames. If you need the original image files rather than a video compilation, look for tools that specifically advertise photo extraction from slideshows.

Why do some TikTok downloaders stop working after the USDS changes?

When TikTok's US operations transitioned to USDS (United States Data Security) ownership in late 2025, the platform updated its API endpoints, content delivery infrastructure, and anti-scraping protections. Downloaders that relied on the old API structure broke immediately. Tools that have been actively maintained -- like AAWebTools and SnapTik -- have adapted to the new infrastructure. Abandoned or infrequently updated tools may still reference deprecated endpoints, which is why they fail or return errors.

How do I download TikTok videos without a watermark?

Most TikTok downloader tools, including , SnapTik, and SSSTik, offer watermark-free downloads by default. They access the original video file before TikTok's watermark overlay is applied. Simply paste the TikTok video URL into the tool, and it will provide a download link for the clean, unwatermarked version. Note that removing watermarks from downloaded content that already has one is a separate process and may raise additional copyright concerns.

Do TikTok downloaders work on mobile phones?

Yes. Browser-based TikTok downloaders like work on any device with a web browser, including iPhones, Android phones, and tablets. No app installation is required. On iOS, downloaded videos are saved to your Files app or Photos library depending on your browser settings. On Android, files typically save directly to your Downloads folder. Some tools like TikMate also offer dedicated Android apps, though browser-based tools are generally safer since they do not require granting app permissions.

What is the difference between a TikTok slideshow and a regular TikTok video?

A TikTok slideshow (also called a photo carousel or photo mode post) is a post containing multiple still images that viewers swipe through, often set to music. A regular TikTok video is a single recorded or edited video clip. Slideshows were introduced in 2022 and became extremely popular for aesthetic content, recipes, tutorials, and storytelling. The key difference for downloading is that slideshows contain individual image files, while videos contain a single video file. Some downloaders can extract both formats, while others only handle standard videos.
