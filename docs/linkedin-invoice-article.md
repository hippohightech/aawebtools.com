## What Makes an Invoice "Professional"

Before comparing tools, it helps to understand what clients and accounts payable departments actually look for. A professional invoice includes:

- **Your business name and contact information** -- including email and mailing address
- **Client name and contact details** -- the legal entity, not just a first name
- **A unique invoice number** -- sequential numbering shows you run a real operation
- **Invoice date and payment due date** -- ambiguity here is the top cause of late payments
- **Itemized list of services** -- descriptions, quantities, and rates for each line item
- **Applicable taxes** -- GST, HST, VAT, state sales tax, or whatever applies to your jurisdiction
- **Total amount due** -- clearly displayed, including and excluding tax
- **Payment instructions** -- bank details, PayPal, Wise, or however you accept payment
- **Payment terms** -- Net 15, Net 30, or your preferred terms

Missing any of these creates friction. Friction leads to delays. Delays lead to cash flow problems that kill freelance businesses.

## Tax Handling Tips for Freelancers

Tax on invoices trips up freelancers more than almost anything else. Here are the basics:

**In Canada**, if your annual revenue exceeds $30,000, you must register for and charge GST/HST. Rates vary by province. In Ontario, it is 13% HST. In Alberta, it is 5% GST only. In Quebec, you charge both GST (5%) and QST (9.975%).

**In the United States**, sales tax on services varies wildly by state. Some states do not tax services at all. Others tax specific categories. Research your state's rules or consult a tax professional.

**In the European Union**, VAT rules depend on whether you are selling to consumers (B2C) or businesses (B2B). The reverse charge mechanism means you often do not charge VAT on B2B cross-border invoices, but you still need to note it on the invoice.

**In the United Kingdom**, VAT registration is mandatory once your taxable turnover exceeds 90,000 GBP. The standard rate is 20%.

When in doubt, add a line item for tax and make the rate configurable. Tools like AAWebTools let you add [multiple tax lines with custom labels and rates](https://aawebtools.com/invoice-generator/), which handles most jurisdictions without issue.

## Pro Tips That Get You Paid Faster

After years of freelancing and talking with hundreds of independent professionals, these are the habits that consistently reduce payment delays:

**1. Invoice immediately.** The moment work is delivered, send the invoice. Every day you wait adds days to when you get paid.

**2. Use Net 15, not Net 30.** Research from FreshBooks shows that invoices with shorter payment terms get paid an average of 8 days faster. Net 15 creates appropriate urgency without being unreasonable.

**3. State your late payment policy upfront.** A simple line like "A 1.5% monthly interest charge applies to invoices unpaid after 30 days" is standard and legally enforceable in most jurisdictions.

**4. Number invoices sequentially.** Never use random numbers. Sequential invoicing (INV-001, INV-002) signals professionalism and makes your records audit-friendly.

**5. Follow up on day one past due.** A polite email on the day payment is due is not pushy. It is professional. "Hi [name], just a quick note that invoice INV-2026-012 was due today. Let me know if you need anything from my side to process payment."

**6. Offer multiple payment methods.** The fewer barriers between your client and their ability to pay, the faster money moves. Include bank transfer details, PayPal, and Wise if you work internationally.
